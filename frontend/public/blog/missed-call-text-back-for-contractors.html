Missed Call Text-Back: The Easiest Way Contractors Stop Losing Jobs

By Stephen Butler · April 2026 · 5 min read

← Back to Blog

You're 30 feet up a ladder replacing gutters. Your phone buzzes in your pocket. By the time you're back on the ground, the voicemail is already sitting there — or worse, there's no voicemail at all. Just a missed call from a number you don't recognize.

That was probably a $400 job. And there's a good chance they already called the next contractor on the list.

This happens to contractors 3–5 times per week. At $300–$500 per job, that's $126,000 in lost revenue every year — from missed calls alone. Not from bad reviews. Not from a bad website. Just from being busy doing your job.

What Is Missed Call Text-Back?

Missed call text-back is exactly what it sounds like: when someone calls you and you don't answer, they automatically get a text message within 90 seconds.

The message looks something like this:

"Hey! Sorry I missed your call — I'm with a client right now. What can I help you with? I'll get back to you as soon as I'm free."

That one text does something powerful: it keeps the lead warm. Instead of hanging up and dialing the next contractor, they reply with what they need. Now you have a conversation going — on your terms, when you have a free moment.

Why It Works So Well for Contractors

Most marketing strategies require time, creative energy, or a big budget. Missed call text-back requires none of those things. It just runs in the background, 24/7, every time you miss a call.

Here's why it's the highest-ROI tool we've seen for contractors:

  • Speed wins. Studies show that leads contacted within 5 minutes are 9x more likely to convert than leads contacted after 30 minutes. A 90-second text response beats almost every competitor.
  • It meets people where they are. Most people prefer texting to leaving voicemails. When you respond via text, you get replies.
  • It works while you're working. You don't have to change your workflow. The system handles it automatically.
  • It's personalized enough to feel real. The text comes from your local business number — not a toll-free automated line.
